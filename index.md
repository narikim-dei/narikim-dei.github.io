# Dr. Nari Kim, Ph.D.

### Geographer of Care, Aging, and Justice

## About

I am a critical geographer whose work examines dignity, justice, and exclusion across the spaces of aging, disability, and care. My research traces how society "produces" old and sick bodies, and how care workers and care receivers navigate political, emotional, and spatial structures that too often marginalize them. Drawing on bio-politics, feminist ethics of care, and critical geopolitics, my scholarship sits squarely within questions of equity and inclusion — even when it isn't always labeled that way: from theorizing eldercare justice and ageism, to my undergraduate thesis on the spatial exclusion of the visually impaired from public space.

I hold a Ph.D. in Geography from the University of Delaware (2023), where I was a member of the [Embodiment Lab](https://sites.udel.edu/geog-bodylab/).
